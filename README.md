# App Empire — can you beat the giants fairly?

A single-file browser game for Year 10 cybersafety. Students run a new social media
startup through 9 big design decisions (the feed, money, notifications, streaks, likes,
endless content, fake news, privacy, screen time). Each decision offers a dark-pattern
option, a middle ground, and a fair-play option — with the psychology behind each trick
and the real-world story it comes from.

**Play it here:** https://wesdlpteam.github.io/App-Empire/

## The goal

Win all three at once: **800M users · making money · Wellbeing 60+**. Two out of three
proves nothing. Dark patterns grow fast but fill your Trouble meter (surprise audits are
dice rolls — your Trouble % is exactly your chance of getting busted). Fair play builds
trust the giants can't copy.

## Classroom use

- One playthrough takes about 10–15 minutes; works on any school laptop, no install or login.
- The ending screen gives students a **design blueprint** of the app they built — the
  starting spec for designing their own Ethical Social Media App.
- Built-in discussion questions ("Talk about it") close the lesson.

## Notes for teachers

- The game is deliberately tuned so that clicking the gentlest option every time falls
  just short ("small but beloved") — winning takes a thought-through mix of principled
  and engaging choices. Dark-pattern-heavy runs collapse.
- Complications (a celebrity live-stream, a hacking attempt) are dice rolls whose odds
  come from the student's own choices — e.g. the less data you collect, the less a
  breach can hurt you.

Made at Wesley College. Content references public reporting on real platform design
